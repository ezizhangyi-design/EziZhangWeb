Video Artwork Description

In a hyper-informational and technologically governed urban space, the structure of perception is undergoing a continuous process of alienation. Blackcurrant No.1, a 3-minute and 30-second abstract animation, is my response to the dissolution of perception in the contemporary metropolis. The film unfolds in chapters of consciousness, each attempting to map the psychic tension and ontological dissipation experienced by individuals navigating the digital thicket.

Chapter I: "Wandering as a Bodily Vehicle" presents a state of corporeal alienation. The body is no longer an active mediator between self and world, but has become a passive vessel, deprived of agency in the symbolic flow. It drifts ghost-like through the urban perceptual mechanism‚Äîboth present and redundant, embedded in the field yet unable to intervene. The body, once the site of lived experience, now functions as a mute conduit, estranged from the formation of meaning.

Chapter II: "Cranial Climax" focuses on the internal turbulence of the information age. Here, "climax" refers not to pleasure, but to the collapse of the subjective world under the weight of perceptual oversaturation‚Äîa threshold at which the individual, overwhelmed by daily data surges, loses the ability to integrate sensory input. This rupture marks a dislocation of inner rhythms and a distortion of temporal perception.

The title Blackcurrant No.1 emerged from a chance event: while seated before my screen, I consumed a blackcurrant-flavored candy and spontaneously assigned it a designation. This object became a perceptual trigger, a conduit for escaping reality and accessing an alternate cognitive dimension. Tangible yet ritualistic, it symbolizes a portal to otherness.

Combining afterimage-like rhythms, geometric structures, and displaced light and shadow, the film seeks to re-mediate perception, reconstructing the interrelation between body, technology, and spatiality in the digital age. Each frame functions as a delayed fold of viewer experience‚Äîan instance of rasterized consciousness in the ongoing process of digital subjectivation.
