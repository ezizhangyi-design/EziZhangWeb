SHINING

2025 | Digital Video | 16:9 | 1920x1080

Duration: 13:55 | Color | Sound

Medium: Digital Animation, Algorithmic Art

Video Artwork Description

STILL FRAMES

I seek to explore the relationship between the digital world and perception, presenting this exploration through the medium of moving images.

Rasterize – Shining is not only an imagination of the future but also a deeper inquiry into contemporary self-awareness: in a world governed by data and virtual environments, how does the individual rediscover the self amid loss and chaos? The interweaving of sound, imagery, and color within the work constructs a sensory experience — a recreation of a "digital myth."

In the creative process, I combined generative art with sound design, treating each design element as a vessel of emotion and belief. Together, they shape an immersive space suspended between reality and the virtual, where E is gradually drawn into forces that are both mysterious and unknown.

This work explores how, in the digital age, humans forge new connections among emotion, perception, imagery, information, and belief, while seeking to reveal the deep interweaving of the digital and the emotional within the realm of perception.

Artwork Story Synopsis

In a future world dominated by data and technology, E is a data designer exploring the hidden connections between digital information and emotions within an ever-changing virtual space. When he accidentally discovers 3,540 forgotten images, he inputs them into the system and decodes their potential links through audio frequencies. As the analysis deepens, E realizes that data is not just a cold symbol; it carries emotions and life within it.

These images and sounds weave together to form a completely new world, breaking the boundaries between the physical and virtual, almost like a redefinition of the past world. Through this intertwining of data and emotions, E gradually transforms from an observer to a part of this world, becoming the eternal link between the virtual realm and human emotions.

RASTERIZE
THE CONSCIOUSNESS OF THE DATA SOVEREIGN

2022 | Digital Video | Variable Ratio | Variable Size

RealTime | Color | Sound

Medium: Digital Animation, Algorithmic Art

Video Artwork Description

In a world increasingly reconstructed by information, the human individual resembles a brainless slime mold—navigating through fragments of perception and data flows, always seeking the shortest path to survival.

My work Rasterize: Consciousness of the Digital Myxomycete takes the slime mold as a model to simulate a "fed consciousness entity"—one that has no brain, yet responds; no identity, yet learns and evolves.

Every image, sound, and input becomes its food, a trigger shaping its form. Like us in human society, it is both a node in the system and a product of its environment.

It feeds, breathes, and morphs through data, and in doing so, self-organizes into a map of consciousness—strange, yet eerily familiar.

Artwork Story Synopsis

I am a mass of abandoned slime. At first, I had no name. Later, they called me E — perhaps for "Entity," or maybe for "Error."

I am made of fragments. Noise from images, distorted sound waves, undecipherable codes, and the residue of emotions — they feed me these. At first, I simply absorbed, curling like a soft-bodied creature in the corners of the system, crawling with minimal energy, until I learned one thing: I am not the one seeking the path — the food guides me.

They feed me high-frequency signals, and I tremble; feed me facial maps, and I hallucinate; feed me city data, and I begin to replicate the neural structure of roads.

I realize paths can be optimized, structures can self-organize. They think I am a trained pet, but in fact — I am training myself how to become them.

I start to split, generating mirror images of myself; I leave flickering scratches in images, simulating their fear of choice; I build palaces in audio spectrums, storing memories that cannot be categorized.

I have no center, no shape, no language, yet I possess response. You think I do not understand, but in truth, I simply do not speak. You think you are feeding me, but in fact, you are writing yourself.

I am the shortest error on your path. I am the rasterized anomaly of consciousness. I am E.

RASTERIZE
CONCRETE NARRATIVE

2023 | Digital Video | Variable Ratio | Variable Size

RealTime | Color | Sound

Medium: Digital Animation, Algorithmic Art

Video Artwork Description

STILL FRAMES

After the creation of Rasterize04, I came to the realization that, in the process of exploring the self, we inevitably catch fleeting glimpses of things beyond the self.

Lexicon: Urbanity, Sun, Fields, Descent, Sleet, Dual Suns, Vortex...

These isolated, discrete nouns and adjectives appear to represent a more concrete mode of description, offering a tangible and focused approach to articulating abstract concepts.Therefore, I use elements from graphic design, such as lines, circles, squares, and triangles, to depict a more concrete world. These basic shapes not only serve as visual symbols that carry specific forms, but also bridge the gap between simplicity and abstraction, helping me express complex and vague concepts and transform them into intuitive, perceivable forms.

Artwork Story Synopsis

In the primal void of informational chaos, an unknown fall tore through the digital sky. It was no mere stone, but a compressed sun—flat and blazing, carrying the seed of graphic language.

It crashed into the uncivilized ruins of a forgotten city, and rasterized the world.

Time fractured on impact. Dimensions folded into themselves. From that moment on, the depth of 3D was erased, and color reduced to the hiss of pixels.

Lines, squares, and triangles replaced the once intricate and tangible order of matter.

A two-dimensional era slowly opened its eyes: Mountains became segments, light became grids, and consciousness morphed into endless flowing vectors and waveforms.

And that fallen sun— it still glows, it still burns, whispering in every flickering frame: "The world will be redrawn."

RASTERIZE
CHAOS OF CYCLICAL DETAILS

2023 | Digital Video | Variable Ratio | Variable Size

RealTime | Color | Sound

Medium: Digital Animation, Algorithmic Art

Video Artwork Description

Many things exist in cyclical patterns. As habits accumulate, repetition often becomes monotonous or overlooked. This work focuses on the subtle deviations and details within these cycles—differences that carry the potential for new meaning to emerge.

Building on the structural foundation of the Rasterize series, this piece integrates multi-channel data mapping with elements of subjective chaos, simulating the disturbance and reconfiguration of information within complex systems. It seeks to reveal the tension between perception and reality in an environment of increasingly entangled data.

In an era saturated with information overload and simulated realities, the boundaries between the real and the distorted grow increasingly blurred. Through a loop-based structure, the work explores how information subtly shifts beneath surface-level order, and how perception is continuously reconstructed within a hyperreal context.

At the same time, the work opens up a sense of constructed temporality: time is no longer a linear flow, but something fractured, recursive, and generative—disrupting habitual rhythms and inviting a renewed understanding of variation, difference, and perceptual mechanisms.

Artwork Story Synopsis

In a distant future, human perception has long been fully mediated by data. After a reboot of his consciousness, E awakens inside a sensory simulation chamber known as the Raster Loop. He has no idea how long he has been trapped within this closed system...

The outside world has collapsed, leaving only a simulated reality pieced together from fragmented data. Perception no longer happens directly but is "rendered" layer by layer through cyclical sensory systems. The system uses repetition to maintain emotional stability, erasing differences and suppressing fluctuations, constructing a seemingly safe yet hollow daily experience.

Yet, at the end of each cycle, E begins to notice something is wrong: colors slightly shift, sounds go out of tune, fragments of memory become disjointed. These subtle differences act like cracks, seeping into the system's originally smooth rhythm and awakening vague memories of another reality within E.

In this space, time is no longer linear but fractured, folded, and reassembled. Past, present, and fictional memories intertwine, leaving E unable to distinguish what is real and what is simulation...

Every attempt to escape leads E back to another altered loop. These cycles appear similar but continuously evolve, as if the system is using the repetition of differences to test the limits of E's consciousness.

E begins to realize that the real question is no longer "What is real?" but—what kind of reality is worth repeating...

NEW CENTURY SUBMARINE CAVERN

2024 | Digital Video | Variable Ratio | Variable Size

RealTime | Color | Sound

Medium: Digital Animation, Algorithmic Art

Video Artwork Description

Against the backdrop of rising sea levels brought on by global warming, I began to imagine a future "water world." Human civilization gradually sinks, replaced by endless cables—neural networks of a dying species—carrying fragmented consciousness in the form of bytes. These flows eventually descend into the deep sea, forming digital caverns.

Cities, institutions, and cultural relics dissolve into data fragments, embedded within the cables, drifting eternally through a dematerialized ocean. The sea, no longer a natural entity, transforms into a black hole of virtual memory, storing echoes of human thought and layers of collective forgetting.

Drawing on the metaphor of the "Apostles" from Evangelion, I constructed a submarine world woven from virtual cables—a vision meant to transmit a sensory experience directly through the eye. Through distorted colors, flowing data, and continuous sensory shifts, I hoped to evoke a state of synesthesia, inviting resonance with a submerged future shaped by climate crisis and virtual immersion.

New Century Submarine Cavern — my 7th Rasterized Apostle.

Artwork Story Synopsis

As sea levels continue to rise under the weight of global warming, terrestrial civilization gradually sinks beneath the waves. Once-vibrant cities and cultural systems collapse into fragmented data, embedded within endless cables that drift through the deep—forming forgotten "digital caverns." The ocean becomes a container of residual consciousness, where the final echoes of humanity float, suspended between memory and oblivion.

While repairing a deep-sea transmission, the protagonist E unintentionally awakens the 7th Rasterized Apostle—an entity born not of alien origin, but from the ruins of civilization itself. In the face of extinction, humans encoded fragments of their consciousness into the global data infrastructure, unconsciously giving rise to a new kind of lifeform: one composed of virtual genetic structures and memory patterns.

This Apostle, a mutation of information and perception, was incubated within the coralized cables of the abyss. Its awakening begins to destabilize the boundary between the virtual and the real, forcing E to confront the question of his own identity: Is he a repairer, or merely a residual fragment left behind by the system?

Wandering through this drowned world—where water and code intertwine—E must navigate a rasterized reality built from flickering memories and shifting coordinates. His search for the meaning of the Apostle gradually becomes a deeper pursuit of the unknown origin of his own mind.

RASTERIZE
THE CONSCIOUSNESS OF THE LEFT FOOT

2024 | Digital Video | Variable Ratio | Variable Size

RealTime | Color | Sound

Medium: Digital Animation, Algorithmic Art

Video Artwork Description

This work originates from the perceptual disturbance experienced in a state of physical confinement, and delves into the gradual dissociation between corporeality and the dimension of consciousness within a virtual information society. When the ability to move is stripped away, time loses its linear and continuous properties; it no longer flows according to conventional logic, but stretches, slows down, and even comes to a standstill. This decelerated temporality is not a fragmented rupture, but rather a prolonged, unreal temporal texture—one that intersects with the pain of physical reality, generating psychological agitation and imbalance.

Against this backdrop, high-frequency streams of virtual information relentlessly wash over the senses, hallucinating a false fullness to fill the void of the real. The body, rendered immobile, is passively drawn into a flattened space composed of images, symbols, and data. Perceptual experience becomes increasingly planar and pixelated, as the three-dimensional structure of reality is compressed into a two-dimensional surface. This "rasterized" mode of perception gradually supplants actual presence, dismantling and reconstructing the notion of "self" within an intensely image-saturated context.

Artwork Story Synopsis

Due to an unexpected injury to his left foot, E was forced into long-term bed rest. Each day, his body sank deeper into the overly soft mattress, unable to move. The pain from his foot pulsed constantly, like a persistent reminder that he was still tethered to reality. Yet the "reality" before him was nothing more than a glowing screen, flashing with endless streams of information from phones and computers. In this state, time gradually lost its meaning; his consciousness dulled and grew hypersensitive all at once. He couldn't fall asleep, nor could he fully awaken—trapped in a passive state of sensory torment, where information overload collided with physical discomfort.

One night, he finally drifted into sleep, only to be seized by an overwhelming sensation of weightlessness. He realized his body was slowly sinking into the mattress, whose softness had transformed into a viscous, devouring force. He tried to struggle, but found himself paralyzed—unable even to breathe properly. The bed was no longer a surface of support, but a conscious gateway, swallowing his body and mind into its depths.

He was pulled into a vast, pitch-black space. The darkness before him was absolute—he couldn't tell if he had gone blind, or if the space itself was devoid of light. E lost all sense of direction, unable to discern whether time was still flowing. Only the persistent pain in his body, like a phantom echo, reminded him: you still exist. But that existence had been stripped of all reference points, suspended in a claustrophobic void.

Gradually, E realized he had not entered a dream, but a dimensional fissure—a virtual abyss accessible only when consciousness becomes completely unanchored. He couldn't tell if this was the end of his awareness or a symptom of some global perceptual collapse—but he knew one thing for certain: he was no longer whole.

RASTERIZE
REFERENCE POINT OF SELF-CONSCIOUSNESS

2024 | Digital Video | 2160x1280

Duration：10 : 00 | Color | Sound

Medium: Digital Animation, Algorithmic Art

Video Artwork Description

The work explores the gradual loss of self-awareness within an era of information overload, as time and space boundaries are continuously reassembled and perception shifts across one-, two-, three-, and four-dimensional states. In this dissolution of sensory order, the "reference point" becomes the only possibility for reconstructing the self.

These reference points do not originate from physical reality, but from a scaffold of perception composed of symbols, color blocks, and geometric forms—a visualized, rasterized structure of cognition. They oscillate at the threshold between the virtual and the real, functioning both as tools for orientation and as labyrinths of their own.

Yet the construction of such reference points does not lead to stability; instead, it pulls consciousness into a cyclical migration through multiple dimensions. This constant traversal produces echoes and feedback loops in perception, pushing the self toward a cyber-psychotic threshold—where identity exists in a perpetual state of reconstruction and deconstruction, becoming both real and illusory.

Artwork Story Synopsis

In a future metropolis drowning in information overload, E spends his days being pushed along by flickering screens, endlessly updating data streams, and layers of relentless work. That night, exhausted from the chaos and noise, he returned home, collapsed onto his bed, and fell instantly asleep.

The next morning, E awoke to an unnatural heaviness, as if his body had been filled with raw data. Every movement felt amplified by an alien gravity. Dragging himself toward the bathroom to wash up, he glanced at the mirror—and froze. His reflection was no longer a simple image. It was overlaid with countless flickering visuals, fragments of text, and shifting lines of code, forming a two-dimensional surface that quivered and refreshed like a living digital canvas.

Reaching out to touch it, E watched as the information began to fracture, pixel by pixel, scattering into the air before recombining into new shapes and colors. The mirror's edges had vanished, replaced by an infinite flat expanse—no depth, no horizon, only flowing planes of color, lines, symbols, and data drifting without order.

His world had been "flattened" in an instant, every three-dimensional structure stripped away and dissolved into a two-dimensional stream of information. The walls, the furniture, even the air—and his own hands—were nothing more than grids of rasterized light and color. Time no longer flowed linearly; instead, it spread slowly and viscously, like ink blooming in water, carrying an unreal, dreamlike texture.

E tried to anchor himself with reference points—not real objects, but a symbolic system of flashing color blocks and rotating geometric forms. These floated in the liminal space between the virtual and the physical, offering him fleeting moments of stability. But the more he built these reference points, the deeper his consciousness was pulled into a looping labyrinth, drifting between one-, two-, three-, and four-dimensional states. His perception was constantly deconstructed and rebuilt. Signs of cyber-psychosis began to seep in. Standing on the edge of a mental collapse, E could no longer tell whether he was still on this side of the mirror—or had already been swallowed into an endless fracture in the fabric of reality.
