Ping Body
A device designed to monitor and analyze the real-time response times between biological organisms and external stimuli. Utilizing a network-inspired "ping" system, the Ping Body tracks the neural and emotional feedback loops within the organism, providing data on the synchronization or lag between thought, action, and response. Originally developed for neuro-synchronization studies, the device was integral in understanding the biofeedback mechanisms between conscious and unconscious states. While its original protocol has been lost, the faint signals still pulse through the system, offering glimpses into its once-central role in cognitive research.

Host Space
Host Space is an intermediary structure anchored in physical reality, designed to sustain the positional reference and feedback of a conscious entity within virtual environments. When cognition is projected into virtual layers, the body still requires a native physical coordinate — Host Space becomes the scaffold of this residual presence.
Legacy systems once relied on Host Space to synchronize user movement and neural feedback. Today, most protocols have shifted toward full cognitive surrogacy. Yet in certain relic devices, the residual frequencies of Host Space still echo — the final trace of memory between the human and the virtual threshold.
